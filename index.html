So you ran seismic ML.
Did it find geology,
or just... patterns?

A friendly reality check for anyone who's ever stared at a colorful facies map and thought, "huh, that looks great - I think."

Here's the thing about seismic machine learning: it is extraordinarily good at finding patterns. The problem is it will find patterns in anything you feed it - including noise, acquisition footprint, and data scaling artifacts. It doesn't know what a channel looks like. It doesn't know what you're looking for. It knows distances and mathematics, and it will give you a beautiful, convincing, colorful result either way.

And before anyone in the back says "this is why I stick to conventional interpretation" - fair point! Good conventional interpretation is built on exactly the right instincts: know your data, trust your geology, check your assumptions. This tool is just those same instincts, applied to the ML workflow. Nothing here requires a statistics degree. Your 30 years of knowing when something seems wrong? That's exactly what this is asking you to use.

"The algorithm found exactly what you gave it. The question is whether what you gave it was geology."

- the whole point of this questionnaire

Twelve plain-English questions. Four topics. No trick questions, no jargon pop quiz. At the end you'll get a clear picture of where your workflow is solid - and a short, specific list of the things worth checking before you put this result in front of anyone.
